Ad Diagnostics Analysis

Ad Diagnostics by Market

Average Ad Diagnostics Metrics

Creative Performance Insights

  • Regional Performance Contrast: GCC markets show significantly stronger ad diagnostics (Saudi Arabia 86-90%, UAE 87%, Kuwait 83-87%) compared to European markets (except UK), with Germany (50-56%) and France (57-61%) showing the weakest performance. This 25-30 percentage point gap highlights how cultural relevance dramatically impacts creative effectiveness.
  • European Market Nuances: Within Europe, a clear hierarchy exists with UK significantly outperforming (78-81% across metrics) other European markets. The "Relevance" metric is particularly weak in Italy (53%) and Germany (50%), suggesting Ramadan messaging lacks cultural resonance despite high media investment in these markets.
  • Investment-Performance Paradox: Despite receiving the highest media investment, European markets show both the lowest Media Efficiency Scores (0.4-0.9 MES) and weakest ad diagnostics, creating a "double inefficiency" where expensive media delivers creatively ineffective content. In contrast, India achieves exceptional performance in both dimensions (5.0 MES, 89-94% diagnostics) with moderate investment.
  • Metric-Specific Patterns: The "Attention vs. Action" relationship varies by region - GCC and India markets show strong motivation metrics (84-92%) that match or exceed attention metrics, while European markets show equal or lower motivation compared to attention, indicating content may grab attention but fails to drive consideration.

Brand Impact Score

The Brand Impact Score (BIS) is a composite metric that combines brand performance metrics (60%) and ad diagnostics (40%) to provide a comprehensive assessment of brand impact in each market.

Brand Impact Score by Market

Brand Impact Score Components

Brand Impact Insights

  • Market Performance Tiers: Markets cluster into distinct performance tiers - India leads with exceptional brand impact (77.4), followed by GCC markets (70-75), UK (65.2), and finally other European markets (50-55). This tiering provides clear prioritization for future investment.
  • Component Analysis: Top-performing markets show balanced strength across all components, while lower-performing markets show particular weakness in relevance and brand association. Germany and France score below 50% on relevance, creating a significant drag on their overall scores.
  • Investment Efficiency: When compared with investment levels, India delivers the highest BIS per dollar invested, while European markets show the lowest return on investment. This suggests significant opportunity for investment reallocation to maximize overall brand impact.
  • Regional Patterns: GCC markets consistently deliver strong brand impact despite moderate investment levels, highlighting the importance of cultural relevance in campaign effectiveness. The UK significantly outperforms other European markets, suggesting potential for market-specific creative approaches.

BIS Methodology

The Brand Impact Score combines seven weighted metrics:

  • Brand Performance (60%): Ad Recall (25%), Brand Association (25%), Ad Appeal (10%)
  • Ad Diagnostics (40%): Attention (10%), Information (5%), Relevance (15%), Motivation (10%)

Formula: BIS = (0.25 × Ad Recall) + (0.25 × Brand Association) + (0.10 × Ad Appeal) + (0.10 × Attention) + (0.05 × Information) + (0.15 × Relevance) + (0.10 × Motivation)

Score Interpretation: 80-100 (Exceptional), 70-79 (Strong), 60-69 (Good), 50-59 (Moderate), Below 50 (Weak)

Investment Analysis

Investment vs. Brand Metrics by Market

Investment Efficiency by Market

Key Investment Insights

  • Regional Investment Performance Analysis: European markets receive the highest investment (UK: ~$874K, Germany: ~$650K) but show the lowest brand metrics (Ad Recall 12-22%, Brand Association 29-42%). In contrast, India receives modest investment (~$278K) yet delivers exceptional efficiency (5.0 MES) and strong brand metrics. GCC markets receive moderate investment with balanced performance and stronger brand metrics (37-67% recall, 20-74% association).
  • Cultural Relevance & GCC Market Insights: GCC countries show unique engagement patterns during the Ramadan campaign due to the cultural significance of the holy month. Despite moderate investment levels, these markets demonstrate stronger brand metrics compared to European markets, suggesting higher receptivity to Ramadan-themed messaging. Performance varies across GCC countries (UAE: 37% recall, 74% association vs. Saudi Arabia: 42% recall, 67% association), indicating different content preferences even within culturally similar markets.
  • Strategic Investment Optimization: Significant potential exists for investment reallocation from low-efficiency European markets to high-efficiency markets like India and Armenia. Additionally, the data suggests that investment in GCC countries during Ramadan campaigns yields better returns on brand metrics, indicating an opportunity to leverage cultural relevance for improved campaign efficiency.
  • Creative Strategy Implications: Different markets show varying relationships between Ad Recall and Brand Association, suggesting different creative approaches may be more effective in different markets.

Strategic Recommendations

Strategic Investment Reallocation

High-Growth Markets: Increase investment in India (+40%) and Armenia (+300%) based on exceptional efficiency metrics and strong brand impact.

Optimization Markets: Reduce investment in European markets (UK -30%, France/Germany -50%) while improving creative relevance.

Expected outcome: 35-40% improvement in overall brand impact efficiency with potential to free ~$820K for reallocation.

Market-Specific Creative Strategy

European Markets: Develop market-specific creative approaches addressing the 25-30% relevance gap identified in ad diagnostics.

GCC Markets: Leverage cultural resonance with deeper Ramadan-themed storytelling to capitalize on strong motivation metrics (84-92%).

Implementation: Create market-specific creative briefs based on component analysis from Brand Impact Score.

Cultural Relevance Optimization

Relevance Gap: Address the 30-35% relevance gap between GCC markets (83-87%) and European markets (50-58%) with market-specific cultural adaptations.

UK Differentiation: Leverage UK's stronger performance (78% relevance) as a template for European market adaptations while maintaining cultural authenticity.

Motivation Metrics: Focus on improving the "motivates to find out more" metric in European markets where it lags attention metrics by 5-10%.
